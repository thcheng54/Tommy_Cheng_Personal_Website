Urbannoyance: Installation

This project is an installation designed to draw attention to the environmental costs of rapid urbanization and pollution.

Concept and Motivation

While urban expansion brings economic growth, it severely exploits nature. Our motivation stemmed from issues like Hong Kong's "world-leading" light pollution (9 times the global standard, mainly from LED/neon signs), which destroys habitats and washes out stars, as well as noise pollution and increased fossil fuel consumption.

The goal was to help participants regain a connection with nature and offer an imaginary visualization of a future, fully urbanized Hong Kong.

The Installation's Goal

The installation was designed to give participants a taste of the peaceful rural side of the city. We wish to convey that even though urbanization is inevitable, there remains a fundamental human need to feel comfort and peace in a land enclosed from constant human intervention.
